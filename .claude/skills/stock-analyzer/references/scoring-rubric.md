# Conviction Scoring Rubric — 0 to 10

Every stock analyzed gets a conviction score at the end of the report. This rubric defines exactly what earns each point. The score must be shown with a breakdown — not just a number.

A score of 10 is not perfection — it means every factor aligns and the setup is as strong as it gets. A score of 6 is the minimum threshold for a BUY NOW or WATCH recommendation.

---

## Scoring breakdown (10 points total)

### 1. Event confirmation (0 or 1 point)

**1 point:** The catalyst event has a **confirmed, specific date** from a primary source (company press release, FDA calendar, SEC filing, exchange filing). The date is ≤ 14 days out.

**0 points:** No confirmed date, event is speculative ("expected sometime in Q3"), or event is > 14 days out.

> Note: Events 15–60 days out can still be WATCH recommendations — they just don't earn this point.

---

### 2. Probability estimate (0, 1, or 2 points)

**2 points:** Base rate + modifiers puts probability ≥ 70% AND there is specific supporting evidence (positive AdCom vote, strong Phase 3 p-value, 4/4 earnings beat history, etc.).

**1 point:** Probability estimate 50–69% with reasonable supporting evidence, OR ≥ 70% but the estimate is based on base rate alone with no specific evidence.

**0 points:** Probability < 50%, or the event type has no reliable base rate and the estimate is pure speculation.

---

### 3. Asymmetric payoff (0 or 1 point)

**1 point:** The bull case upside is at least **3x the bear case downside** in dollar terms on a 30% position.

Example: Bull case = +$180 on $240 position. Bear case = -$60. Ratio = 3:1. Earns the point.
Example: Bull case = +$50. Bear case = -$120. Ratio = 0.4:1. Does not earn the point.

**0 points:** Risk/reward is less than 3:1 in favor of the bull case.

---

### 4. Small float / market cap amplifier (0 or 1 point)

**1 point:** Public float < $500M at time of entry. Small float means a given dollar amount of buying creates a larger % move. This amplifies the upside scenario.

**0 points:** Float ≥ $500M. The stock can still be a valid play — it just won't produce the 300–1000% moves that a small-float name can.

---

### 5. Short squeeze potential (0 or 1 point)

**1 point:** Short interest ≥ 15% of float. This means a positive catalyst forces short covering on top of normal buying — doubles or triples the expected move size.

**0 points:** Short interest < 15%. Normal move dynamics apply.

---

### 6. Catalyst quality — supporting evidence (0, 1, or 2 points)

**2 points:** Strong, specific, pre-existing positive evidence for the catalyst outcome. Examples:
- AdCom already voted ≥ 7-3 in favor
- Unblinded interim data released and it's positive
- Phase 2 was strongly positive (p < 0.001, large effect size)
- Company has 4/4 earnings beats + raised guidance every quarter + new product launching
- Peer company in same indication just got approved by same FDA division

**1 point:** Some positive evidence but incomplete or ambiguous. Examples:
- Phase 2 positive but modest (p = 0.03, borderline effect size)
- Company has 3/4 earnings beats
- AdCom hasn't been held yet, but no safety signals raised in FDA correspondence

**0 points:** Little to no pre-existing positive evidence. The catalyst is genuinely uncertain with no precedent or prior data to lean on.

---

### 7. Cash runway / no dilution risk (0 or 1 point)

**1 point:** Company has ≥ 12 months of cash at current burn rate, AND no open shelf registration that could be used to dilute shareholders around the event. Dilution near a catalyst destroys the upside.

**0 points:** Cash runway < 12 months, OR an open shelf registration or at-the-market (ATM) offering program exists. Capital raise risk is present.

---

### 8. Smart money confirmation (0 or 1 point)

**1 point:** At least one of the following is present:
- Insider open-market purchase (cash buy, not option exercise) within the last 90 days
- Specialist biotech/sector hedge fund (Baker Bros, RA Capital, Perceptive, Boxer Capital, etc.) opened or significantly increased a position in the last quarter's 13F
- Unusual call option buying at strikes above current price within the last 2 weeks

**0 points:** No insider buying, no institutional buildup, no unusual options activity. Smart money is not visibly positioned.

---

## Total and interpretation

| Score | Label | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| 9–10 | **Exceptional setup** | BUY NOW — size up to 30% of portfolio |
| 7–8 | **Strong setup** | BUY NOW — standard 20–30% sizing |
| 6 | **Acceptable setup** | BUY NOW (cautious) or WATCH — 15–20% sizing |
| 5 | **Borderline** | WATCH — set alert, revisit closer to event date |
| 3–4 | **Weak setup** | SKIP — risk/reward not favorable enough |
| 0–2 | **Poor setup** | SKIP — do not enter |

---

## How to present the score in the report

Always show point-by-point, not just the total:

> **Conviction score: 7/10**
>
> | Factor | Points |
> |---|---|
> | Event confirmed ≤ 14 days | ✅ 1/1 |
> | Probability ≥ 70% with evidence | ✅ 2/2 |
> | Asymmetric payoff ≥ 3:1 | ✅ 1/1 |
> | Small float < $500M | ✅ 1/1 |
> | Short interest ≥ 15% | ❌ 0/1 — SI only 8% |
> | Catalyst supporting evidence | ✅ 1/2 — Phase 2 positive but modest |
> | Cash runway ≥ 12 months | ✅ 1/1 |
> | Smart money confirmation | ❌ 0/1 — no insider buys, no institutional buildup |
> | **Total** | **7/10** |

This format makes the score transparent and contestable. If Abbas disagrees with a point allocation, he can challenge it directly.

---

## Override rules

These override the score regardless of the total:

- **Halal failure:** Automatic SKIP regardless of score. No exceptions.
- **No confirmed event date and score < 7:** Automatic WATCH, never BUY NOW — don't enter pre-catalyst plays blind on timing.
- **Negative EV:** Automatic SKIP even if conviction score is high. A 90% chance of +5% and a 10% chance of -80% is negative EV.
- **Company needs to raise cash before the event:** Automatic 1-point deduction from final score (dilution wipes upside).

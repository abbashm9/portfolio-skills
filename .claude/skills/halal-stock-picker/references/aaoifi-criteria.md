# AAOIFI Halal Screening Criteria

The Accounting and Auditing Organization for Islamic Financial Institutions (AAOIFI) Shariah Standard No. 21 governs halal stock screening. Every pick must pass these tests.

## Two-stage screening

### Stage 1: Business Activity Screen (Qualitative)

A company is HARAM (outright excluded) if its core business is in:

- **Alcohol** — production, distribution, retail
- **Gambling** — casinos, lotteries, betting platforms
- **Pork and non-halal food** — production, processing
- **Conventional banking and insurance** — interest-based finance, conventional insurance
- **Adult content / pornography**
- **Tobacco** — production and distribution
- **Weapons and defense** — offensive weapons specifically
- **Music and Shariah-non-compliant entertainment** (some scholars debate this)
- **Interest-based financial services** of any kind

A company in an *otherwise permissible sector* can still earn incidental impure income (e.g., interest on cash deposits, leasing space to a non-compliant tenant). The 5% threshold (Stage 2) handles this.

### Stage 2: Financial Ratio Screen (Quantitative)

Three ratios, each measured against the company's **market capitalization** (not total assets):

| Ratio | Threshold | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Interest-bearing debt** | < 30% of market cap | Limits exposure to riba-based financing |
| **Interest-bearing securities** (cash + cash equivalents + deposits) | < 30% of market cap | Limits the company acting like a cash-holding entity |
| **Non-permissible income** | < 5% of total revenue | Limits incidental haram income |

If ANY of these is breached, the stock fails — regardless of how clean the core business is.

## Important nuances

- **Market cap, not total assets.** Some screeners (FTSE, MSCI) use total assets instead. AAOIFI is stricter — it uses market cap, which is more responsive to share price moves.
- **Compliance changes quarterly.** A stock that passes today can fail next quarter if it takes on more debt or share price drops sharply.
- **Apple example:** Apple passes AAOIFI but sits at ~4.14% impure income because of Apple Music, Apple TV+, and Apple Card interest. This is *near the ceiling* and warrants dividend purification.
- **Purification:** Even on compliant stocks, the impure income portion of any dividends received should be donated to charity to "purify" the income.

## Standards comparison

Five major standards exist. AAOIFI is the strictest among the commonly used ones:

| Standard | Debt measure | Cash measure | Impure income |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AAOIFI** | <30% of market cap | <30% of market cap | <5% of revenue (includes interest income) |
| **S&P / DJIM** | <33% of 24-36mo avg market cap | <33% | <5% (excludes interest income) |
| **FTSE** | <33% of total assets | <33% of total assets | <5% |
| **MSCI** | <33% of total assets | <33% of total assets, AR <33.33% | <5% |
| **Musaffa** | Uses AAOIFI primarily, ranks across all 5 | | |

When in doubt, use AAOIFI — it's the strictest and most widely accepted in the Gulf region.

## Verification sources

Use these to verify halal status before any pick:

- **Zoya** (zoya.finance) — AAOIFI-based, free tier available
- **Musaffa** (musaffa.com) — Multi-standard ranking, grades A+ through F
- **Islamicly** — Popular in South Asia / Middle East
- **halal.sh** — AAOIFI Standard No. 21, with full citation explanations
- **HalalScreener** (halalscreener.app) — Pass/fail with detailed grade

Always cite the source and date of the most recent compliance check.

## When in doubt

If a stock's compliance is borderline or disputed:

1. Default to excluding it
2. Don't try to make a religious ruling — refer the user to a qualified Islamic finance scholar
3. Note the borderline status clearly so the user can decide

# Exit Strategy Reference

The exit plan matters more than the entry. Most retail losses come from having no plan to sell.

## Stop-Loss Sizing by Volatility

Match stop width to the stock's normal range:

| Stock Type | Beta range | Stop width |
|---|---|---|
| Defensive (LLY, JNJ, MRK, ABT) | <1.0 | -6% to -8% |
| Mega-cap quality (NVDA, AAPL, MSFT) | 1.0–1.5 | -7% to -9% |
| High-beta growth (AMD, AVGO, ASML, TSM) | 1.5–2.0 | -8% to -10% |
| Cyclical / parabolic (MU, semis at top of cycle) | 2.0+ | -10% to -12% |

## Take-Profit Ladder Structures

### Conservative (small move expected, ~15–25%)

- TP1: sell 50% at +12%
- TP2: hold 50% to +25% or trailing stop

### Standard (medium move, 25–40%)

- TP1: sell 30% at +12%
- TP2: sell 40% at +25%
- TP3: hold 30% for +40%+

### Aggressive (large move, earnings/catalyst-driven)

- TP1: sell 30% at +15%
- TP2: sell 40% at +30%
- TP3: hold 30% for +50%+

## Catalyst Exits

For each pick, identify the binary event that defines the trade:

- **Earnings:** Decide BEFORE the print whether you're holding or trimming. Common strategy: sell 30–50% the day before earnings, hold the rest.
- **Product launch:** Exit if launch data disappoints (efficacy below expectations, mixed reviews, delayed timeline).
- **Regulatory event:** FDA decisions, antitrust rulings, export controls — exit on adverse outcomes regardless of stop level.
- **Sector rotation:** If the entire sector breaks down, exit even compliant winners.

## Time-Based Exits

If the trade is dead money, redeploy:

- If position is flat (±3%) with no catalyst for **30 days** → trim 50%
- If position is flat for **60 days** with no upcoming catalyst → exit entirely

## Universal Override Rules

These trump everything else:

1. **Halal compliance breach** — Re-screen quarterly. If a stock drops out of AAOIFI compliance (debt or impure income breaches thresholds), sell at market open the next trading day regardless of P&L.

2. **Single position >45% of portfolio** — A winner that runs hard can become concentration risk. Trim back to original target weight.

3. **Portfolio down >8% from start** — Stop new entries. Move to cash. Re-evaluate. A bad month is fine; a bad month plus revenge trades is how accounts blow up.

4. **Never average down on a stop-out** — If a stop hits, it hits. Re-entering is fine after a new setup forms, but no doubling down on losers.

5. **Geopolitical tail risk on TSM** — Any credible Taiwan Strait military escalation (PLA exercises encircling Taiwan, US naval response) → sell TSM at market open immediately. This is binary risk you can't hedge.

6. **Parabolic warning on MU and cyclical semis** — If MU or similar memory/cyclical name has an -8% single-day drop on no news after a parabolic run, that's likely the cycle top. Sell remaining position that day, don't wait.

## Cancel Order Rules (for limit orders not yet filled)

A limit order is an invitation, not a commitment. Cancel if:

### Universal cancel triggers

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Stock gaps up >3–5% before fill | Cancel — you're chasing |
| Stock breaks below stop-loss level before fill | Cancel — setup invalidated |
| Negative thesis-breaking news | Cancel immediately |
| Earnings pre-announcement / warning | Cancel — wait for actual print |
| VIX spikes above 25 | Cancel both new entries, wait for vol to compress |
| Fed surprise hawkish pivot | Cancel growth/momentum entries |
| Halal status changes | Cancel immediately |
| Day before earnings, still not filled | Cancel — don't enter hours before binary event |
| Not filled within 5 trading days | Cancel — stock is running away |

### One rule that matters most

**If the reason you wanted to buy disappears, cancel the order — even if it's "almost filled."**

People hold onto orders because they're anchored to "the price I wanted to pay." That's not investing, that's stubbornness.

## Order Placement Mechanics

- **Stop-losses:** Always GTC (Good-Til-Cancelled) in the broker. Don't rely on yourself to manually exit in panic.
- **Take-profits:** Set as price alerts, not auto-sells. You want manual control on the upside — sometimes you let it run.
- **Position entries:** Scale in over 1–3 days for high-volatility names. Buy all at once for stable names.
- **Review cadence:** Friday after close. Daily checking is how you fumble winners.

## Pre-Earnings Decision Template

For every position holding into an earnings event, write down the answer BEFORE the print:

1. **Hold full position through earnings?** Only if conviction is extreme and you can stomach a -15% gap.
2. **Trim 30–50% the day before?** Most common. Locks gains, keeps upside exposure.
3. **Exit completely before print?** When risk/reward is asymmetric to the downside.

Whatever you decide, decide it BEFORE you see the price reaction. Decisions made in the moment of a -10% gap are almost always wrong.

## Trade Journal Recommendation

For every position, write down:

- Entry price and date
- Why you entered (thesis in 1 line)
- Stop-loss level and exact $ amount you'll lose if hit
- Take-profit levels
- Catalyst date
- What would invalidate the thesis

Review weekly. If you can't answer "why am I still in this trade," you shouldn't be.

# Rotation Playbook — Daily Check

Since Abbas isn't withdrawing money, freed capital from trims should be redeployed. This file guides when and how to suggest rotations.

## When to suggest a rotation

### 1. After TP1/TP2 trim
A position hit a take-profit level. The freed cash needs a home.
- **Default:** suggest keeping in cash 1–3 days to wait for setup
- **If a high-conviction halal name has a fresh catalyst within 5 days:** suggest redeploying immediately

### 2. New high-conviction name emerges
- Halal-screened, passes 4+ of the 5 pillars (valuation, growth, financials, momentum, catalyst)
- Suggest trimming a winner (NOT a loser) by 20–30% to fund a starter position

### 3. Dead-money rotation
- Position flat ±3% for 30+ days with no catalyst → trim 50%
- Position flat for 60+ days → exit and rotate

### 4. Sector rotation
- If a sector (e.g., AI semis) is up 30%+ in 60 days, trim concentration risk
- Rotate into uncorrelated halal sectors (healthcare, software, consumer)

## How to format rotation suggestions

Always include exact mechanics:

> 🔄 **Suggested rotation:**
> - **Sell:** 0.11 NVDA shares at market (~$25)
> - **Buy:** 0.05 ASML shares at market (~$25)
> - **Why:** ASML reports earnings in 6 days; EUV monopoly with no near-term competition. Diversifies away from NVDA single-name concentration.
> - **Risk:** ASML can disappoint on Q2 GM guide; been volatile post-print historically.
> - **Your call.** Confirm before I help draft the order.

## Aggressive rotation rules (since no withdrawal needed)

- Up to **30% of any single position can be trimmed in one suggestion** if a clear better opportunity exists
- Up to **2 rotations per week** to avoid overtrading
- Always keep at least **4 names in the portfolio** for diversification (no single-name concentration above 50%)
- Never rotate INTO a position if its halal status hasn't been re-verified within the last 90 days

## Rotation target priority — catalyst plays first, large-cap fallback

### Primary: Catalyst Radar (Step 3.5)

The first and preferred rotation target is always the top result from the Catalyst Radar in Step 3.5. These are live-scanned, near-term binary plays sourced from the FDA calendar, biotech readout trackers, and earnings surprise setups. They are NOT pre-set — they change every day based on what's actually in the market.

**Use the Catalyst Radar result if conviction ≥ 6/10 and halal status is verified (or verifiable before entry).**

Position sizing: up to **30% of total portfolio value** per catalyst name. Combined catalyst exposure cap: 50% of portfolio. See `references/catalyst-scanner.md` for full sizing and entry timing rules.

## Rotation triggers calendar

Maintain awareness of upcoming catalysts that could trigger rotations:

| Window | Watch for |
|---|---|
| Pre-earnings (5 days before) | Decide hold/trim/exit |
| Earnings day | If beat + raise → consider adding; miss → exit |
| 1 week post-earnings | Confirm reaction is sustained, not a one-day spike |
| Fed meetings | Hawkish surprise → trim growth/momentum |
| Major hyperscaler capex updates (MSFT/META/GOOGL/AMZN earnings) | Read-through to NVDA, AVGO, ASML, AMD |
| GLP-1 trial readouts | Read-through to LLY, NVO |

## Anti-rotation rules

Don't suggest rotation when:

- A position is within 2% of stop-loss (let the stop work, don't pre-empt)
- Market is in a confirmed downtrend (S&P below 50-day MA for 5+ days) — preserve cash instead
- VIX > 25 (volatile environment, wait for compression)
- Halal compliance hasn't been verified on the target name in 90+ days

## Final principle

The best rotation is often "no rotation." Suggest moves only when the expected return on the new position clearly exceeds what the existing position is doing. Default to inertia — frequent trading erodes returns through slippage, taxes, and emotional decisions.

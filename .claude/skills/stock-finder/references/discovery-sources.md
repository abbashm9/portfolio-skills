# Discovery Sources — Where to Find Catalyst Plays

This file documents the specific sources the stock-finder uses, what each one surfaces, and how to extract actionable candidates from it. These are the places where upcoming catalysts are announced before mainstream media picks them up.

---

## Tier 1 — Highest signal sources (search these every run)

### 1. FDA PDUFA Calendar

**What it is:** The FDA publishes Prescription Drug User Fee Act (PDUFA) action dates — the legal deadline by which the FDA must make a decision on a drug application. These are announced months in advance and are fully public.

**What to search:**
- `FDA PDUFA calendar [month] [year]`
- `site:biopharmcatalyst.com PDUFA upcoming`
- `"PDUFA date" [month] 2026 upcoming decisions`

**What you're looking for:** Any NDA or BLA with a PDUFA date within 45 days for a company with:
- Market cap < $2B (smaller = bigger % move)
- Indication with ≥ 70% base approval rate (or strong modifiers)
- Float < $500M (amplified move)

**Key insight:** PDUFA dates are announced 60–90 days before the decision. The stock usually starts moving 2–4 weeks before — but pre-PDUFA entries 3–6 weeks out still capture most of the pre-catalyst drift plus the event upside.

---

### 2. AdCom (Advisory Committee) Schedules

**What it is:** Before some FDA decisions, an independent advisory committee votes publicly on whether to recommend approval. The vote is public and is heavily correlated with the final FDA decision.

**What to search:**
- `FDA advisory committee meeting [month] [year] scheduled`
- `"AdCom" biotech [month] 2026`
- `FDA advisory committee calendar upcoming`

**What you're looking for:**
- Upcoming AdCom votes (the vote itself is a catalyst — stocks move +/-20–40% on the day)
- Previous AdCom results where a stock hasn't yet received its PDUFA decision

**Key insight:** A positive AdCom vote is one of the strongest pre-PDUFA signals available. An 8-2 vote in favor historically leads to FDA approval ~80%+ of the time. Finding a post-positive-AdCom stock still trading below pre-AdCom levels is a rare high-conviction setup.

---

### 3. ClinicalTrials.gov — Phase 3 Completion Dates

**What it is:** All clinical trials registered in the US must report their estimated completion dates on ClinicalTrials.gov. "Primary completion date" = when the primary endpoint data will be collected = when results readout.

**What to search:**
- `site:clinicaltrials.gov "phase 3" "primary completion date" [month] [year]`
- `"phase 3 results" expected [month] [year] biotech readout`
- `"top-line data" OR "interim analysis" expected [month] [year]`

**What you're looking for:** Phase 3 trials with primary completion dates in the next 30–60 days for:
- Indications with established precedent (not first-in-class with no comparator)
- Companies with market cap < $1B
- Trials that have already passed an interim analysis (de-risks the event)

**Key insight:** Phase 3 readouts are volatile but not random — a company doesn't announce a readout is coming without having strong enough Phase 2 data to justify the Phase 3. The data quality from Phase 2 is a public record on ClinicalTrials.gov.

---

### 4. SEC EDGAR — 8-K and 10-K Filings

**What it is:** Companies are required to file 8-Ks (current report) for material events. Companies often disclose upcoming catalyst timelines in quarterly 10-K/10-Q filings.

**What to search:**
- `site:sec.gov "data readout" OR "PDUFA" OR "phase 3" 8-K [current month]`
- `"expected to report" OR "anticipated in" 2026 phase 3 data SEC filing`
- `SEC EDGAR "clinical data" expected [current quarter] 2026`

**What you're looking for:**
- 8-Ks announcing upcoming catalyst timelines that haven't yet been widely picked up
- 10-Q/10-K disclosures of expected data readouts in the next 1–2 quarters
- Companies disclosing FDA acceptance of an NDA application (PDUFA clock starts here)

**Key insight:** SEC filings are the primary source. When a company files an 8-K saying "we expect to report Phase 3 top-line data in Q2 2026," that's the most reliable catalyst date available — it comes directly from management.

## Tier 2 — High signal, run every run

### 5. Unusual Options Activity

**What it is:** Options flow that is disproportionate to the stock's average volume — specifically, large call buying at strikes well above the current price, often with short-dated expiry. This is frequently informed money positioning before a catalyst.

**What to search:**
- `"unusual options activity" [today's date] OR [this week] small cap`
- `"unusual call buying" [current week] biotech OR pharma`
- `unusualwhales OR "flow aliens" unusual options [today]`

**What you're looking for:**
- Large call sweeps (single large orders vs. multiple small retail orders)
- Calls expiring within 30 days of a known catalyst date — this is someone betting on a specific event
- High put/call ratio inversion (more calls than usual puts = bullish positioning)

**Key insight:** Options flow is the most real-time smart money signal available. When a $100M fund buys $500K in call options on a $200M market cap company expiring in 3 weeks, they know something. They're not always right — but the frequency of this preceding big moves is significant.

---

### 6. Insider Buying Clusters

**What it is:** SEC Form 4 filings report insider transactions within 2 business days. A cluster of insiders (CEO + CFO + director, or multiple executives) buying open-market shares in the same 2–4 week window is statistically significant.

**What to search:**
- `"insider buying" cluster multiple executives [current month] 2026`
- `SEC Form 4 open market purchase [current month] small cap biotech`
- `"insider purchase" [current month] [current year] pre-catalyst`

**What you're looking for:**
- Open market purchases (cash buys) — NOT option exercises or granted shares
- Multiple different insiders buying within the same 30-day window
- Insider purchases at prices similar to or above the current stock price

**Key insight:** A CEO buying $500K of their own stock on the open market 6 weeks before a Phase 3 readout is about as strong a signal as legally exists. They can't buy on material non-public information — but they can buy when they're genuinely confident in their own drug's data.

---

### 7. High Short Interest Catalysts

**What it is:** Stocks with very high short interest (> 20% of float) that have an upcoming positive catalyst. When the catalyst hits, shorts must cover at any price — this forces a squeeze on top of the underlying move.

**What to search:**
- `"high short interest" upcoming catalyst [current month] 2026`
- `"most shorted" small cap catalyst binary event 2026`
- `"short squeeze" setup catalyst [current month] biotech`

**What you're looking for:**
- Short interest > 20% of float
- A confirmed upcoming catalyst (FDA, earnings, trial readout)
- Low borrow availability (makes covering harder and more expensive)

**Key insight:** Short squeezes are the mechanism behind many of the 500%–1000% single-day moves. The shorts built up because consensus was that the drug would fail or the company would miss. When they're wrong, the forced covering creates a feedback loop. You don't need the squeeze to happen — you just need the underlying catalyst to be positive and the squeeze is optionality on top.

## Tier 3 — Supplemental signals

### 8. Biotech Catalyst Tracker Sites

Sites that aggregate upcoming biotech catalysts in one place. Good for cross-referencing and finding anything missed in direct source searches.

**Search:** `biotech catalyst tracker [current month] [next month] 2026`

Known aggregators to specifically search for:
- BioPharma Catalyst
- Evaluate Pharma calendar
- Adam's Calendar (biotech specific)
- Seeking Alpha biotech catalyst tracker

---

### 9. Pre-Market Unusual Volume With Catalyst

**What it is:** Stocks moving significantly pre-market (>5%) with a specific catalyst disclosed, not just random movement.

**What to search:**
- `"premarket movers" catalyst [today's date]`
- `"premarket gainers" FDA OR "phase 3" OR earnings [today]`

**What you're looking for:** Stocks with confirmed catalysts that are starting to move — entering before the full day session captures the continuation.

**Key insight:** This is the most time-sensitive signal. Pre-market moves with catalyst backing often extend significantly during the regular session. But it requires acting fast.

---

## What to ignore

These sources produce noise, not signal. Avoid them for discovery:

- **Reddit (r/wallstreetbets, r/stocks):** By the time something is popular there, the move has happened. Retail flows in last.
- **Instagram/Twitter stock influencers:** Same issue. They're posting what already moved, not what's about to move. This is what Abbas is trying to get away from.
- **CNBC/Bloomberg top stories:** Large-cap coverage, already priced in. Not the edge.
- **Stock screener "top gainers today":** Looking backwards. Useful for confirmation but not discovery.
- **Analyst upgrades/downgrades on large caps:** The upgrade is priced in within minutes of publication. No edge.

The edge is always in the **calendar** — finding events before they happen, in stocks that aren't being watched by the crowd.
